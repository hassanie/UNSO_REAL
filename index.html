UNSO

United Nation Student Organization

The United Nations Student Organization is an interdisciplinary organization that aims to challenge and stimulate the understanding of international relations through the intricate and complex lens of the United Nations. UNSO provides the ability of its members to publish their critical analysis of UN actions, passed resolutions, peacekeeping operations, and so forth. In addition to networking events, UNSO fuels debate by entertaining panels of professionals from various fields, such as global relations, environmental studies, and human rights. Lastly, its main objective is to train its members on MUN style debating protocol and diplomacy, and to give them the opportunity to attending external conferences to perform the skills obtained, some of which include HarvardMUN, WorldMUN & McMUN.

Samir Janmohamed

Director of Internal Affairs

I am in my fourth year pursuing a double major in Economics and International Development Studies. My hobbies include reading, photography, discussing foreign and domestic policy in Canada and my native Tanzania in addition to writing. Often I do all this while indulging and critiquing the flavour of strong cups of coffee during my travels around the world. I’ve been involved in MUN from the time I was in high school at the Aga Khan Academy, Mombasa. During this time, I had the opportunity to attend East African MUN held at the UN Headquarters in Nairobi, Kenya. After a brief break, I continued my MUN career in my second year at York, representing the society at Queens Model United Nations Invitational and the University of Toronto Mississauga Model United Nations. Having had the opportunity to spend a year abroad at the University of Nottingham, I was able to attend conferences including Oxford International MUN, Leeds MUN(Crisis chair), Durham MUN, York MUN and London International MUN. I received awards for best delegate at DurhamMUN and YorkMUN in addition to a Diplomacy Award, the only award available, at LiMUN. Over this time, I have amassed experience in a number of committees including the WHO, ECOFIN, Crisis, G8+BRICs and Security Council representing countries from both the Global North and South. My hope is to channel my international MUN experiences into all the participants of the UNSO over the course of this academic year to create competent speakers and future leaders of the world.
